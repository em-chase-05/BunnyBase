Grooming

Rabbits are animals that groom themselves! They are relatively clean animals. However, they cannot get all their hair out on their own, and a rabbit eating too much of their hair can cause health concerns. So you as the rabbit owner need to routinely groom your rabbit!


Brushing Your Rabbit

A rabbit's hair needs to be brushed as they cannot get out all the hair they need to when shedding their coats on their own. A brush built for rabbits is the best for getting all your rabbits loose hair (it will be metal with rubber in between the bristles, so that it will grab the loose hair). Rabbits need to be brushed at least once a week to avoid your rabbit consuming to much of their hair. Rabbit's can't vomit like a cat can so overconsumption of hair can cause health concerns.
Molting is the big shedding time for rabbits. They molt twice a year, once to shed their summer coat and once to shed their winter coat. During molting, you might need to brush your bunny for frequently for a period of time. Additionally, more frequent grooming is necessary for specific breeds, such as lionheads, angoras, and other long-haired rabbits.

Clipping Your Rabbit's Nails

A Rabbit needs their nails to be trimmed.You should check every four to six weeks to see if your rabbit's nail need to be clipped. Nail clipping can be a stressful situation for a rabbit, to reduce stress, wrap them in a towl. This will lessen the rabbit's ability to squirm, so you can do their nails faster and they won't injure themselves or you.
You want to make sure to not cut in the quick! Look at the part of the rabbit's nail that looks like a pocket of blood, that's the quick! Trim above the quick, to avoid pain or bleeding. If your rabbit's have darket nails, shine a light under the nail to see the quick.

Bathing a Rabbit? Please Don't

Rabbits do not need to be bathed. Rabbits are naturally clean, and they clean themselves through licking. Bathing is not necessary unless they often get poopy bottoms, and even then try to avoid submerging your rabbit in water as much as possible. Rabbits have more difficulty regulating their body temperature and submerging them in water makes it more difficult for them to do that. If you need to bathe a specific part try using pet safe wipes or a spray bottle and paper towels. If only a part of your bunny is dirty try wetting that specific part, as minimizing that amount your bun needs to regulate his body temperature. Additionally, bathing is stressful for your rabbit and stress which can cause health issues.
