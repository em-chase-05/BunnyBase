Behavior

You want to make sure your rabbit is happy and content! The way to know is by watching their behavior. Rabbits are different then say a dog or a cat in showing how they feel. In many ways it is more subtle, they don't bark or meow. However, once you know the signs, reading your rabbit's behavior becomes intuitive! And knowing how your rabbit feels is important in giving your bun the best life possible!


Common Rabbit Behaviors

  • Binky: A binky is a common term for a happy hop a rabbit does! A rabbit will jump and twist in the air, this shows that your rabbit is happy and wants to play!
  • Biting: Biting can mean multiple different things with a rabbit. A small nip is usually a sign of your rabbit wanting your attention. However, a rabbit biting hard is a sign of fear or stress.
  • Ears on Alert: A rabbits ears can be a sign into how they feel! Ears that are up can mean that your rabbit is hearing something that has their attention. However, ears back means you rabbit is very relaxed.
  • Grinding Teeth: Grinding teeth when petting your rabbit can mean a sign of contentment. Be mindful of loud grinding because that could mean your rabbit is in pain.
  • Licking: Licking is a sign of affection! It is one of the best signs to tell that your rabbit feels safe with you and loves you.
  • Thumping: Thumping is when your rabbit stomps his back foot. A rabbit thumping could be communicating a possible danger. Additionally, it can be a way of a rabbit expressing frustration and anger.
  • Flopping: A rabbit flopping is a sign of contentment. They often do this before they relax or take a nap. They will flop on their side or their back!

An Affectionate Rabbit

Some affectionate behaviors in a rabbit is the rabbit lowering their head/presenting their head to be pet. Additonally, licking is a way that rabbits show affection to humans and other rabbits. Nose nudging is also a common way for rabbits to show affection. It is your rabbit's way of saying "Hello!"

A Conent Rabbit

Toothclicking/grinding teeth is a clear sign of a content rabbit. Rabbit's will lay down stretched out with feet behind them when they are content. This also a sign that they feel safe! Flopping is also another way your rabbit shows that they feel safe and content. Additionally, sleeping near you shows that they feel safe and happy.

A Curious Rabbit

Rabbits can be very curious! They will bring their ears forward and lean their body forward to see what is going on. They also will stand up on their haunches to get a better view of the space because they are unsure and are curious of what is happening!

A Happy Rabbit

Rabbits will run and jump when they are happy! They also will binky to show they are happy and feeling playful. They also will show their playfulness with a quick shake of the head! Additionally, pushing and tossing objections shows they are happy and playful. A bunny will also circle a human to show that they love them.

A Fearful Rabbit

Thumping can be a sign of a scared rabbit or that a rabbit is frustrated. Scared rabbits will also run and hide to feel more safe. Rabbits don't have much facial expression, however, you can tell how they feel in their eyes. A rabbit having eyes that are bulging can be a sign of fear or stress.

A Rabbit In Pain

A sign your rabbit is in pain is loud teeth grinding, hunched up or not moving during regular acitivty, eating less or refusing to eat, dragging stomach on the floor, or eyes bulging.

An Aggressive Rabbit

A sign of an agressive or defensive rabbit is a stance where the rabbit has their ears back, chin forward, and tail up. A rabbit grunting or growling is a sign of aggression. Biting is a clear sign of a rabbit being on the defensive. They will also bat with their front paws on their haunches in a boxing stance.

A Territorial Rabbit

Rabbits, like many animals, have sign of being territorial of the people or things they love. Chinning is a clear sign of a rabbit saying you are mine, as they are putting their scent on you through chinning you. A more messy form of a rabbit marking it's territory is scattering poop. This can be discouraged by spaying/neutering, and keeping the areas they are in clean.
