Housing & Food

Housing and food is a key part of keeping your rabbit happy and healthy! They need enough space to run, and healthy food give them the energy to do so.


Housing

Where should my rabbit stay?

There are multiple ways to give their rabbit their own space in your home. Everyone's space is different so make sure you have enough space for your rabbit to feel comfortable.
Free-roaming is the best way to house your rabbit and is what we reccomend. Giving them space to roam and run is vital for their well being. However, we recognize that free-roaming is not ideal for everyone. Another good option in a pen, in which your rabbit can be let out to run with you when you are home and able to watch them.
Additionally, house your rabbit in a room that doesn't get too hot, as rabbits do best at around 55-72 degrees fahrenheit.

What should their space have?

A rabbit needs multiple things in their space to keep them happy and healthy! A litter box is very necessary, we reccomend using a cat sized litter box as rabbit sized litter boxes tend to be too small. Your liter box needs well Litter! There are many different types of litter that your rabbit can use, however we reccomend not using clay litter, which if consumed can cause health concerns. We reccomend using paper or wood based litter, either shaves or pellets. Avoid using cedar or pine based litters!
Rabbits also need food and water, so having food and water bowls in their space is necessary. Bowl are suggested for water than bottles as it is easier to clean and easier for the rabbits to drink. The water should be changed once a day and the bowl should be washed every few days.
A hiding place is something that is reccomended for a rabbit to have in their space. Rabbit can get stressed, scared, or frustrated, and they need a place to run away to to feel safe and calm down. They, unfortunately, sometimes don't want to talk with you and need their own space for a bit. A rabbit having that safe space can help them regulate their emotions and have a more happy bunny.
Enrichment is important for rabbits. A rabbit should have various toys and other activities to do, so they don't get bored and start chewing on your furniture. There are many different types of enrichment for rabbits, so research and choose what is best for you.

Food

A Rabbit's Diet

Rabbits have a pretty straight forward diet, but each part of it is important for them to have a balanced one. A rabbit's diet consists of hay, hay pellets, fruits, and vegetables. And of course a few treats!

Hay

Hay makes up 80%-90% of a rabbit's diet. Grass hays are reccomended for rabbits, such as timothy, orchard grass, brome, and oat hay. Additionally, buy the freshest hay possible for your rabbit! Alfalfa hay is not a good choice for an adult rabbit, however, it can be given to rabbits under a year old, but they should be switched to grass hay once turning one.

Hay Pellets

Hay pellets are apart of a rabbit's diet in order to get extra nutrients! Adult rabbits, of an average size, only need 1/4 cup of pellets daily. If a rabbit is under five pounds, they can be fed a 1/8 of a cup. You do not need to feed bigger rabbits more as it is not the main part of their diet.
Alalfa Pellets can be given to rabbits under one years old, but make sure to feed grass hay with alalfa pellets if you chose to give them to your rabbit.
Not all rabbit food is made the same! Doo not buy rabbit pellets with dried corn, nuts, and seeds. Those can cause potential health concerns if apart of their diet.

Vegetables

There are a lot of vegetables that are safe and good for rabbits to have in their everyday diet! It is reccomended that rabbits have 2 cups of fresh vegetables per 6 pounds of body weight for adult rabbits. Dwarf breeds and rabbits under 5 pounds should get only one.

Fruit

Fruit is also apart of a rabbit's diet! However, it is given to them sparingly. You should only give your rabbit small amounts of fruit once or twice a week.
Fruit that your rabbit can eat are: apple, banana, berries, cherries, grapes, melon, nectarine, orange, papaya, peach pear, pineapple, plum watermelon.

Treats

Give your rabbit treats sparingly, healthy treats are recommended such as fresh fruit or hay based treats. Oxbow brand rabbit treats and dried flowers are also recommended.
Not all rabbit treats are made equal! Avoid treats with added sugar, preservatives, and artificial coloring, and never giver your rabbit human treats.

Food Do Nots

Do not give your rabbit: all human treats, beans, beet greens, cabbage, cauliflower, cereal, chocolate, corn or corn-cob, crackers, iceberg lettuce, legumes, mustard greens, nuts, pasta, peas, potatoes, rhubarb, seeds, sugar, turnip greens, yogurt.
